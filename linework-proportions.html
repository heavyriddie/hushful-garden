Elegant Line Work - Proportion Studies

Optimizing layout for progression visualization

A: Intimate Garden Focus

Large gardener (18%), narrow gate opening, dense flower beds close to viewer. Emphasizes personal connection - you ARE in the garden. Flowers dominate, tree is backdrop. Best for: showing flower variety and detail.

B: Grand Tree Showcase

Wide gate opening, dominant central tree with dramatic growth progression. Smaller gardener (12%) for scale. Tree goes from tiny sapling to magnificent canopy. Best for: dramatic before/after, single focal point.

C: Balanced Composition

Equal weight to tree, flowers, and gardener. Medium proportions throughout. Winding path draws eye through all elements. Growth affects everything evenly. Best for: holistic progress representation.

D: Path Journey

Wide, prominent winding path as central element. Gardener walking the path. Flowers line the journey. Emphasizes progress as a journey, not destination. Best for: representing ongoing health journey.

E: Layered Depth

Strong foreground framing, mid-ground garden, distant tree. Each layer grows independently based on different metrics. Creates rich parallax depth. Best for: multi-metric visualization (CGM, ketones, activity).
