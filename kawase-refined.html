Kawase Woodblock - Art Quality Variations

5 approaches to improving the visual quality

A: Precise Botanicals

Accurate flower shapes with proper petal arrangements. Five-petal roses, hydrangea clusters, iris sword-leaves. Simple geometry but botanically correct. Bold Kawase colors with refined natural forms.

B: Bokashi Gradients

The signature Kawase technique - smooth gradated washes within flat shapes. Sky transitions, shadows on stone, tonal depth in foliage. Each element has subtle internal gradation while keeping crisp edges.

C: Layered Silhouettes

Strong depth through overlapping silhouette layers. Dark foreground framing, mid-tones in middle, atmospheric distance. Bold value contrast creates dramatic depth like traditional prints.

D: Elegant Line Work

Clean outlines and key lines define shapes, like woodblock print edges. Flat color fills with deliberate black outlines on key elements. Minimal but precise linework adds definition and clarity.

E: Paper Texture

Subtle washi paper texture composited over the final render. Creates authentic woodblock print feel with minimal compute. Slight grain and fiber patterns suggest traditional printing methods.
